I am Ruoceng Xu (but you can call me Oliver), a Year 4 undergraduate student in Mathematics at Imperial College London. My interest in mathematics is mainly in how algebraically formalized geometry interacts with differential geometry. Current Work

I am currently working on my MRes and a note on differential topology. My undergraduate final project is about connecting variety and circle-manifold rings in an ambitious goal: it aims to define the concurrency variety of Q[x] from scratch.

I’m also keenly interested in global differential geometry, so I have been taking dedicated notes as I progress through advanced topics. I intend to make these notes accessible as a small resource for anyone with Year 2 level or beyond in mathematics.
